Correlations Discovered Between Health Risks and Age and Income, Smoking and Income

While it is clear there is a relationship between Smoking and Poverty, it is not so obvious that there is a clear relationship between Average Age and Poverty. At best, for Average Age and Poverty, one can say that it is less likely to see an older-than 40 year old under the '10' poverty line than it is to see an under-32-year-old.

As for smoking, it is clear that higher-frequency smokers have a higher likelihood of being high in poverty; the converse also applies.
